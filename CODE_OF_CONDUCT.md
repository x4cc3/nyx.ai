# Code of Conduct

NYX is a technical security project, but the community standard is simple: be respectful, be clear, and keep collaboration safe for everyone.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and professional.
- Give actionable, technically grounded feedback.
- Assume good intent unless there is clear evidence otherwise.
- Keep discussion focused on the code, design, documentation, and user impact.
- Respect legal and ethical boundaries around security testing.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Hate speech or discriminatory language
- Doxxing, threats, or unwanted sexual attention
- Sharing credentials, private data, or sensitive third-party information
- Encouraging illegal, abusive, or unauthorized attacks against unrelated targets
- Repeated bad-faith derailment, trolling, or spam

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, and other contributions that do not follow this code of conduct.

Depending on severity, maintainers may also:

- issue a warning
- lock a thread
- close or reject a contribution
- temporarily or permanently block participation

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainer privately through GitHub rather than opening a public thread with sensitive details.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, and other project-managed community spaces.
